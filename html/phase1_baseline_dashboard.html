The baselines converge dramatically faster than the U-Nets — both reach 95%+ in epoch 1 (ConvNeXt 95.8%, Swin 95.3%) while the U-Nets start at 51.6% and 65.1% respectively. This is expected: the baseline head only needs to learn a single linear mapping from a 768-dim pooled vector, while the U-Net decoder must learn to reconstruct spatial structure from scratch across three decoder blocks. By epoch 15 both baselines plateau near 99.4% mean class accuracy, revealing that the pretrained ConvNeXt-V2 and Swin-V2 backbones already contain near-perfect image-level fruit discrimination in their frozen representations. The ~3–4 pp gap between baseline and U-Net at epoch 15 is the cost of pixel-level spatial learning — and phase 2 fine-tuning exists to close it. The U-Nets are still climbing at epoch 15 (not plateaued), suggesting phase 2 will narrow this gap further.
