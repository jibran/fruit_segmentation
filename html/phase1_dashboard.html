analysis

ConvNeXt-V2 leads across all metrics at epoch 15. The 1 pp mIoU gap and 18% lower loss are consistent with the architectural hypothesis: ConvNeXt's 7×7 depthwise convolutions are well suited to detecting local texture patterns like rot and bruising, while Swin's shifted-window attention provides less benefit when global context is not required. Both models show a positive val−train mIoU gap, indicating the augmentation pipeline is working as intended — making training harder than validation rather than causing overfitting. ConvNeXt's convergence is also smoother: Swin shows a brief val mIoU dip at epoch 5 (0.869) before recovering, suggesting its attention mechanism takes longer to find a stable optimum under the frozen-backbone constraint.
