Cell colour: green = high accuracy · red = low accuracy · sorted by ConvNeXt U-Net ascending
Rotten pomegranate and rotten grape are the hardest classes across all models. Both baselines cap at 94.7% on rotten pomegranate (the validation split contains only 19 images of this class), while the U-Nets reach 97.8% and 94.4% respectively — the decoder's spatial context helps distinguish rotten pomegranate from rotten apple which share similar dark-red colouring. Rotten grape at 93.5–96.1% is limited by the small number of validation samples and the visual similarity to rotten jujube. Fresh guava is the weakest fresh class at 94.3% for ConvNeXt U-Net — its yellow-green colouring overlaps with fresh jujube at certain image angles, and the decoder must rely on shape context to separate them.

Rotten pomegranate and rotten jujube are the only classes where the U-Net outperforms the baseline. For every other class, the image-level classifier matches or exceeds the segmentation model at epoch 15. This is expected: the frozen decoder hasn't yet learned to leverage spatial context for these hard classes, while the baseline's global pooling already captures the distinctive texture patterns. These classes will be the ones to watch most carefully in phase 2.
