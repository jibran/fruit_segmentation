Swin gained more from phase 2 (+2.18 pp) than ConvNeXt (+1.31 pp), narrowing the gap from 1.04 pp to just 0.17 pp. This confirms the phase 1 hypothesis: Swin's attention weights benefit significantly from domain adaptation, while ConvNeXt's depthwise convolutions needed less correction. Both models are effectively tied on pixel accuracy at 99.1%. ConvNeXt's val loss is more volatile (spike at epoch 5 to 0.046) while Swin converges more smoothly — if phase 2 runs for the full 30 epochs, Swin may close the remaining mIoU gap entirely. Swin already edges ahead on mean class accuracy at epoch 14 (0.9898 vs 0.9869), suggesting it is catching up on the harder underrepresented classes.
